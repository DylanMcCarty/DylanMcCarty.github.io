How is pseudo-code helpful for programming?


This article is far overdue, yet i believe more experience will provide better insight into this subject. pseudo-code is a very import part of the process of walking yourself through the procedure you intend to design, as a result of this pre-programming process it will take what you know and what you're capable of and expose gaps in what your knowledge, in a sense, pseudo-coding will triangulate the logic your sure of while high-lighting the logic you're not sure of, this will establish a basis for what you'd like your code to look like, and how you're going to lay out the TRICKY process of fact finding and discovery a crucial part of the process, it cuts down on wasted time and wasted man-power, similar to how a runner might walk a track a couple times before running and complete stretches, they warm up and prepare for the stretch of preparing themselves for the real work, similarly if a runner were to run a track without stretching their legs first and moving around in preparation for the sprint, they hurt themselves by not giving themselves the capability to coordinate themselves before trying.
